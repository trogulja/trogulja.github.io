## The phosphor green

There's something about green text on a dark background that feels *right* to developers. It's a callback to the CRT monitors of early computing — phosphor green glowing against black.

This site leans into that aesthetic on purpose. Not because it's trendy, but because it strips away everything unnecessary and lets the content breathe.

## Constraints as features

When you limit your palette to essentially two colors and one accent font, you're forced to make every element earn its place. No decorative gradients, no hero images fighting for attention — just text, structure, and whitespace.

Some design principles behind this site:

1. **Content first** — nothing competes with the words
2. **Fast by default** — static HTML, minimal JavaScript
3. **Readable** — generous line height, comfortable measure, muted text colors
4. **Accessible** — works without JS, respects system preferences

## Light mode exists too

Not everyone wants to stare at a dark screen. The light mode flips the palette — white background with deep green text. Same vibe, different brightness.

The toggle remembers your choice via `localStorage`, so it sticks between visits.

## Keep it simple

The best personal sites are the ones that get out of the way. This is mine.
